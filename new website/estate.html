Step 1 · Estate & Family Protection Readiness Quiz

For each question, choose the answer that best describes your current situation. There is no “perfect” answer — we just want a realistic snapshot.

1. How important is it to you that your family is not burdened or stressed if something unexpected happens to you?
This sets the tone — we want to understand how seriously you view planning ahead.
2. Which best describes your current family situation?
This helps us understand which laws and risks affect you the most.
3. Do you currently have a valid, updated Will or Wasiat that reflects your present family and assets?
This is usually the foundation of any estate plan.
4. Have you legally appointed a guardian to take care of your children if something happens to you?
This decides who steps in for day-to-day care if both parents are no longer around.
5. Have you decided who should manage your children’s money and inheritance until they turn 18?
Guardian and “money manager” can be the same person — or different people.
6. If you have children, do you have at least one son?
This affects how faraid or default inheritance rules may pull in extended male relatives.
4. Does your Will/Wasiat clearly state how your estate should be shared between your spouse and children?
5. For properties (house, apartment, land), is it clear who should inherit or take over each property?
6. Do you have at least one son among your children?
In many families without sons, extended male relatives may inherit under faraid.
4. Are your EPF and insurance nominations updated so your spouse receives what you intend?
5. Is your property ownership structured so that your spouse can continue to stay or benefit from it without major legal difficulty?
6. Have you clearly documented how you want your assets to be divided between your spouse and your parents?
4. Do you know who would inherit your assets if you passed away single, without a Will?
5. Is there at least one trusted person who could practically step in to handle your affairs if something happens to you?
6. Have you written down, even simply, who you want to benefit from your assets?
4. Have you legally appointed someone to take care of your child/children if something happens to you?
5. Have you decided who should manage your child’s inheritance and daily money if you pass away?
6. Does your trusted person know where to find your key documents and how to access emergency funds for your child?
4. Have you updated your EPF/insurance nominations after your divorce or remarriage?
5. Have you updated your Will/Wasiat so it matches your current family structure (ex-spouse, new spouse, children, stepchildren)?
6. For any property still shared with an ex-spouse, have you decided what should happen to it if something happens to you?
4. Is it clearly stated how your estate should be divided among your children or other heirs?
5. Have you taken any steps to reduce the chance of disputes between your children, siblings or other relatives?
6. Does at least one trusted child or relative know where to find your documents and how to handle basic procedures (banks, EPF, property)?
7. Does someone you trust know where to find your important documents?
For example: Will/Wasiat, land title, sale & purchase agreement, insurance policies, loan agreements.
8. Do you have any simple way for someone you trust to access important digital accounts if needed?
For example: email, online banking, investment apps, e-wallets, business platforms.
9. If you have a business, property with others, or complex family structure (divorce / stepchildren), do you have a clear written plan for what should happen?
If this doesn’t apply to you, you can choose Not applicable.
10. Have you clearly shared your main wishes with your spouse, children or parents?
At least in a basic conversation — it doesn’t have to be a big formal family meeting.
